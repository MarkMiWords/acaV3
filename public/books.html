The IVO Trap

Intervention Orders: From the Inside Out

There is no way of knowing how many family violence orders are enforced across Australia. What we do know is how many have wound up in court. In 2023–24, 42% of all civil cases finalised in Australian Magistrates' Courts involved originating applications for domestic violence orders — around 131,000 cases.

This book examines the system from the perspective of those inside it. Not the policy makers, not the advocates, not the commentators — but the people whose lives are reshaped by a single piece of paper.

Why this book exists

The conversation around intervention orders in Australia is dominated by institutions. The courts publish statistics. Advocates push for reform. Media covers the extremes. But the daily reality of living under — or being subject to — an order is rarely heard from the people who experience it first-hand.

The IVO Trap is not an argument for or against the system. It is a record of what the system looks like from the inside out.
