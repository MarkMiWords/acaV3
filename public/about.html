About A Captive Audience

A digital storytelling and media project built around real, first-hand prison narratives. A secure infrastructure designed to bridge the gap between carceral isolation and global publication.

Voice sovereignty The Forge Local-first storage AI-assisted, never AI-authored Accessibility

What is this?

A Captive Audience is a publishing platform and writing workspace for incarcerated and formerly incarcerated people. It gives writers a focused, supported environment to shape their stories without surrendering ownership.

Who is it for?

Justice-affected writers, people living under constraint, and anyone who needs a focused writing environment that lowers barriers without stealing voice. The tools assist. They never author.

Why the Forge?

Writing tools are often expensive, cluttered, or built for people who already have advantages. The Forge is a guided workspace — not a chatbot — that helps you write, revise, and prepare work for publication.

How it works

WRAP: Write, Revise, Articulate, Publish. A structured workflow with local-first storage, safety guardrails, and AI assistance that keeps the writer in control at every stage.

A Captive Audience was originally just a clever name for a Substack channel. It became a publishing house when I realised that self-publishing was the only way to seize sovereignty over our words.

The Forge — coming online in stages

Each stage is built, tested, and deployed before the next begins.

  1. Stage 1: Stable scaffold, Firebase hosting, static pages, domain setup. The foundation everything else stands on.
  2. Stage 2: Writing workspace with local-first Sheets storage and the Partner AI for real-time feedback.
  3. Stage 3: Guided revision modes — Rinse, Wash, Scrub, Polish, Sanitise. Smart editing that respects the writer's voice.
  4. Stage 4: Voice dictation and accessibility enhancements. The Acoustic Link for spoken-word input.
  5. Stage 5: Publishing formats — newspaper, Substack, manuscript. Full mastering and audit pipeline.
